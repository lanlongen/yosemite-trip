🌲 The plan in one line

We leave Mariposa at noon, it's ~1h20 each way to the Valley, and we want to be home by ~6:30 — that's only about 3½ hours in the park. So we're keeping it all in the Valley: no big mountain climb.

👉 An easy valley afternoon: the gift shop, the flat Mirror Lake walk, a meadow stroll, one cool-off river dip, and Tunnel View on the way out. Glacier Point and Tenaya Lake are great — but both blow the clock today (here's why, below).

🪞 Mirror Lake🏊 River dip🚗 Valley only

⏱️ The clock: arrive ~1:20pm → everything's in the Valley → leave by ~5pm → home in Mariposa by ~6:30. Relaxed, with real time for the Mirror Lake walk.

  1. Yosemite Valley gift shop — give it proper time: souvenirs, postcards, a poster. (Village Store + Yosemite Valley Lodge shops.)
  2. Mirror Lake — a flat ~2 mi round trip from Curry Village to calm, reflecting water. A cold wade is fine while it's holding water in early summer.
  3. Cook's Meadow stroll — a flat boardwalk loop in the heart of the Valley, with classic Half Dome and Yosemite Falls views.
  4. Cool off at Sentinel Beach — a sandy Merced River stretch with calm shallows for a cold dip.
  5. Tunnel View on the drive out — the perfect last photo before heading home.

⚠️ Water safety: June water is snowmelt — cold, and the Merced runs faster and higher than it looks. Stay in the calm shallows at the beach areas; never swim near or above waterfalls.

Skipping — busts the clock

Glacier Point Road

The iconic high panoramas — for a longer day.

Glacier Point and Washburn Point are the park's headline drive-up views, with Sentinel Dome and Taft Point hikes right there. We'd love it.

But the climb is ~1 hr up and the return is from the top — about 2¼ hrs back to Mariposa. With a noon start and a 6:30 return, we'd have to turn around almost as soon as we got up there. It needs a longer day, so we're saving the climb.

Skipping — too far

Tenaya Lake (Tioga Road)

The best swim in the park — just not on a day like today.

Tenaya Lake is the park's best true swim — crystal-clear alpine water, a sandy shore, no hike to reach it. We looked hard at it.

But from Mariposa it's about 2.5 hours each way (~5 hours round trip). With a noon start and a hard 6:30 return, that's the entire day in the car for one swim — so we're skipping it. Here's what it looks like, at least.

🏞️ Same goes for Olmsted Point, the other classic Tioga Rd stop (Half Dome from the back) — gorgeous, but out the same long road, and a viewpoint rather than a swim.

Mariposa → SF is ~3–3.5 hrs with traffic and a stop. Re-entering the park tomorrow is optional backtracking (the Valley is east of Mariposa, while SF is west), so today carries the good stuff and tomorrow is best kept as a clean midday departure.

🧭 A few notes

  • Fuel up before the park. No gas in Yosemite Valley — fill in Mariposa or El Portal.
  • Restrooms are at the Village, Yosemite Valley Lodge, and Curry Village (near the Mirror Lake start).
  • Bring water + a towel for the river dip; the beaches have no facilities.
  • Cell signal is patchy — screenshot directions and the pins before you lose service.
