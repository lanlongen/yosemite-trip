Option A

Glacier Point Road

The iconic-views afternoon — drive-up panoramas plus an optional hike.

🚗 ~45–60 min up🏔️ Big views🥾 Optional hike
  1. Valley quick stop — Lodge or Village Store for restrooms, coffee, snacks before the climb.
  2. Drive up Glacier Point Rd (~45–60 min, winding; open in 2026).
  3. Glacier Point + Washburn Point — two drive-up viewpoints minutes apart; the headline panoramas of Half Dome and the Valley.
  4. Sentinel Dome / Taft Point trailhead — one trailhead, two trails: stronger hikers take Sentinel Dome (~2.2 mi round trip, a short steep granite scramble to a 360° summit); others take Taft Point (~1.1 mi each way, flat, to the cliff-edge fissures). Regroup at the car.
  5. Tunnel View on the golden-hour drive back down — the perfect closer.

🅿️ Parking: the small trailhead lot fills fast — midday is the worst window, but it eases by mid-afternoon. If it's full, use the roadside overflow and walk in.

Option B

Tioga Road & Tenaya Lake

The swimming day — the pick if a real swim is the priority. Skip Glacier Point.

🏊 Best swim🚗 ~1.5 hr each way🙂 Low effort
  1. Drive Tioga Rd (~1.5 hrs each way from the Valley). It's open for 2026 and usually less crowded than the Valley or Glacier Point — which helps with a midday start.
  2. Olmsted Point — easy roadside stop: Half Dome seen from the back, plus a short trail beyond the lot if you want to stretch.
  3. Tenaya Lake — a crystal-clear alpine lake right off the road. Park and walk to the sandy shore (no hike), calm water with no current — genuinely swimmable, but cold (it sits at ~8,150 ft).

⚠️ Water safety: June water is snowmelt — cold, and the Merced runs faster and higher than it looks. Tenaya Lake is calmer (no current) and the safer, best true swim; the Valley's Merced beaches are for cooler dips. Never swim near or above waterfalls.

🚻 Heads-up: some Tioga Rd facilities/restrooms may still be limited — check before relying on them, and bring water.

📍 Olmsted Point 📍 Tenaya Lake
Option C

Easy Valley Afternoon

Low effort, no big mountain drive — and a river dip to cool off.

🌳 Lowest effort🏊 River dips🚗 No big drive
  1. Village Store + a meadow stroll — souvenirs, picnic supplies, and flat valley-floor wandering with cliffs all around.
  2. Mirror Lake (optional) — a flat ~2 mi round trip from Curry Village to calm, still water. Can you swim? Yes — no current, fine for a cold wade or dip in early summer while it's holding water (it can shrink to a shallow pond later in the season).
  3. Cool off at the river beaches — Sentinel Beach or Cathedral Beach Picnic Areas: sandy Merced River stretches with calm shallows, good for wading and a dip.
  4. Tunnel View fits neatly on the way in or out.

⚠️ Water safety: June water is snowmelt — cold, and the Merced runs faster and higher than it looks. In the Valley, stay in the calm shallows at the beach areas; avoid anywhere the current is moving, and never swim near or above waterfalls. (For a proper swim, Tenaya Lake in Option B is calmer and best.)

Mariposa → SF is ~3–3.5 hrs with traffic and a stop. Re-entering the park tomorrow is optional backtracking (the Valley is ~1h20 east of Mariposa, while SF is west), so today carries the big stuff and tomorrow is best kept as a clean midday departure.

If you do pop back in tomorrow, keep it to one quick thing — the Village Store or a short, flat Mirror Lake walk.

🧭 A few planner notes

  • Fuel up before the hills. No gas in Yosemite Valley — nearest is Crane Flat, El Portal, or Mariposa.
  • Cell signal is patchy on Glacier Point Rd and Tioga Rd — screenshot directions and download an offline map first.
  • Layers + water. Glacier Point Rd and Tenaya Lake are 7,000–8,000+ ft and run cooler than the Valley.
  • Best light is late afternoon at Glacier Point / Washburn and at Tunnel View.
