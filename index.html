Option A ✓ Recommended

Glacier Point Road + a Sentinel Beach dip

Both worlds — a cold river swim and the iconic high viewpoints.

🏔️ Views🏊 River dip🚗 ~45–60 min up
  1. Arrive in the Valley around midday.
  2. Yosemite Valley gift shop — give it proper time: souvenirs, postcards, a poster or two. (Gift shops at the Village Store and Yosemite Valley Lodge.)
  3. Cool off / picnic at Sentinel Beach Picnic Area on the Merced — sandy beach, calm shallows, picnic tables, a cold snowmelt dip. Cathedral Beach Picnic Area is a near alternative.
  4. Drive up Glacier Point Road (~45–60 min, winding).
  5. Glacier Point + Washburn Point — drive-up viewpoints minutes apart; banked regardless of how trailhead parking goes.
  6. Sentinel Dome / Taft Point trailhead — one trailhead, two trails: park once. Stronger hikers take Sentinel Dome (~2.2 mi round trip, ~400 ft gain, a short steep granite scramble at the top to a 360° summit); others take Taft Point (~1.1 mi each way, flat, to the fissures and clifftop). Regroup at the car.
  7. Tunnel View on the golden-hour drive back down.

🅿️ Parking: the small trailhead lot fills fast — midday is the worst window, but it eases by mid-afternoon (about when you'll have driven up). If it's full, use the roadside overflow within walking distance — don't circle.

⚠️ Water safety: June water is snowmelt — cold, and the Merced runs faster and higher than it looks. Stay in the calm shallows at the beach areas; never swim near or above waterfalls.

Option B

Easy Valley Afternoon

The pivot if energy's low on arrival — no big climb, just an easy day with a dip.

🌳 Lowest effort🏊 River dip🚗 No big drive
  1. Yosemite Valley gift shop — take your time browsing the Village Store and Lodge gift shops for souvenirs.
  2. Meadow stroll — flat valley-floor wandering with the cliffs all around.
  3. Mirror Lake (optional) — a flat ~2 mi round trip from Curry Village to calm, still water. Fine for a cold wade or dip while it's holding water in early summer (it can shrink to a shallow pond later).
  4. Cool off at Sentinel or Cathedral Beach — sandy Merced River stretches with calm shallows.
  5. Tunnel View fits neatly on the way in or out.

⚠️ Water safety: June water is snowmelt — cold, and the Merced runs faster and higher than it looks. Stay in the calm shallows at the beach areas; never swim near or above waterfalls.

Considered — saving for next time

Tenaya Lake (Tioga Road)

The one that got away — for now.

Tenaya Lake is a stunning crystal-clear alpine lake and the best true swimming spot in the park — calm water, a sandy shore, and no hike to reach it. We seriously considered it.

We're skipping it this trip because from Mariposa it's about 2.5 hours each way (~5 hours round trip). With a midday start and an early SF departure tomorrow, that turns into a driving-dominated day for a single swim. We're saving it for a future trip when we're staying inside the park.

🏞️ Olmsted Point is the other classic Tioga Rd stop (Half Dome from the back) — a viewpoint, not a swim. Same reason it waits for next time.

Mariposa → SF is ~3–3.5 hrs with traffic and a stop. Re-entering the park tomorrow is optional backtracking (the Valley is ~1h20 east of Mariposa, while SF is west), so today carries the big stuff and tomorrow is best kept as a clean midday departure.

If you do pop back in tomorrow, keep it to one quick thing — the gift shop or a short, flat Mirror Lake walk.

🧭 A few planner notes

  • Fuel up before the hills. No gas in Yosemite Valley — nearest is Crane Flat, El Portal, or Mariposa.
  • Cell signal is patchy on Glacier Point Rd — screenshot directions and download an offline map first.
  • Layers + water. Glacier Point Rd tops out around 7,000+ ft and runs cooler than the Valley.
  • Best light is late afternoon at Glacier Point / Washburn and at Tunnel View.
