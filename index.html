🗓️ Today vs. tomorrow — do the good stuff today

We leave around midday tomorrow and want an early-ish SF arrival. Mariposa is already on the westward side toward SF, so re-entering the park tomorrow means backtracking — roughly 2.5–3 extra hours of driving in the wrong direction before we even start home.

👉 The play: knock out everything below today. Make tomorrow a clean Mariposa departure.

🚗 The drive home to SF — pad the estimate

Maps will say Mariposa → SF ≈ 2h47. That's the no-traffic, no-stops number. Don't plan on it.

👉 Budget 3.5–4 hours door-to-door once you add a fuel stop, a food/bathroom break, and Bay Area afternoon traffic (the Altamont/I-580 stretch and the bridge approach are the usual snarls).

Returning the rental? Most companies want it back full or charge a steep per-gallon fee. Gas in/near the park is scarce and pricey (no gas in Yosemite Valley). Don't fill in the mountains — top up cheaply about 30–45 min before the drop-off (Tracy / Livermore / Manteca, or right by the airport).

Good food + bathroom + fuel stops on the way: Mariposa (before you leave the hills), then Oakdale or Manteca/Tracy on the CA-99 → I-580 corridor — fast food, clean restrooms, gas.

Buffer for the return: aim to arrive at the rental drop-off 30–45 min before any hard deadline (flight, return time) to cover fueling and the return shuttle. Leaving ~midday → realistic SF arrival mid-to-late afternoon.

🤝 Keeping the group together

We want to minimize splitting up — and one of us has a sprained ankle.

The approach: pick stops where everyone goes to the same place, then people choose different-difficulty options once there. Nobody's left behind, nobody's forced up a trail.

The clearest example is the Sentinel Dome / Taft Point trailhead: the whole group parks together, stronger walkers head to Taft Point (~1.1 mi each way) or Sentinel Dome, while tired or sore folks do a short flat out-and-back on the same trail or relax near the cars. Same lot, same area, no real separation.

🦶 Sprained ankle: every stop below has a flat/short option flagged. The drive-up viewpoints (Tunnel View, Washburn) need zero walking; Glacier Point is short and paved; skip only the full Taft Point hike.

1Yosemite Valley Lodge

🚗 Drive-up🚻 Restrooms🦶 Easy

Kick off in the Valley — gift shop, restrooms, and coffee before the climb, with El Capitan towering overhead and Yosemite Falls a short stroll away. Stretch, top up snacks, then point the car toward Wawona Road.

Sore ankle? All flat and paved — no problem. A good spot to rest up before the viewpoints.

+Mirror Lake (optional)

🦶 Flat~2 mi round trip~100 ft gain

The easiest "real" walk of the day: a flat ~2 mi round trip from Curry Village to a calm lake that mirrors the surrounding granite. Great low-effort option for everyone — and the best candidate if we do a quick re-entry tomorrow.

Sore ankle? Flat and gentle, but 2 mi is still 2 mi — turn back whenever. Heads-up: by late summer the lake can shrink to a shallow pond.

2Tunnel View

🚗 Drive-up🦶 Easy📸 Iconic

The postcard shot of Yosemite — El Capitan, Bridalveil Fall, and Half Dome framed in one sweep, straight from the car park. Hit it on the way up to Glacier Point, or save it for the drive back down.

Sore ankle? Zero walking — the view is right at the lot.

≈ 50 min from Tunnel View (up Glacier Point Rd)

3Glacier Point

🚶 Short paved walk~20 min round trip🦶 Gentle

The big one. A short paved walk (~20 min round trip) opens onto a jaw-dropping panorama of Half Dome, the Valley floor far below, and the high country beyond. Easy underfoot and worth every step — the must-do if you only pick one.

Sore ankle? Paved and gentle — fine taken slowly, with benches along the way. Skip it only if the ankle's really bad; the Washburn Point view (next) needs no walking.
📍 Open in Google Maps
≈ 5 min from Glacier Point (just back down the road)

4Washburn Point

🚗 Drive-up🦶 Easy🏔️ Big view

A drive-up viewpoint a minute below Glacier Point, with arguably the best straight-on profile of Half Dome and a peek at the Merced River's waterfalls. No walking required — pull over, look up, snap away.

Sore ankle? Perfect — the whole view is a few steps from the car. The best "big payoff for zero effort" stop.
📍 Open in Google Maps
≈ 10 min drive from Washburn Pt to the trailhead

5Taft Point

🥾 Hike~1.1 mi each way🦶 Shorter option at trailhead

From the Sentinel Dome / Taft Point trailhead it's about 1.1 mi each way to a thrilling clifftop with the famous fissures and a guardrail over a sheer drop. Huge payoff, but it's a real walk.

⚠️ Parking caveat: the easy ~1.1 mi version is only from the Sentinel Dome / Taft Point trailhead lot — not by hiking from Glacier Point (that's a separate ~9 mi route people confuse it with). The small trailhead lot fills fast; arriving ~2–3pm improves your odds of a real spot vs. a long roadside walk-in.

Sore ankle? Skip the full hike — wait near the trailhead or do a short flat out-and-back on the same trail while the others go. Same parking, group stays together.

🌲 A few planner notes

  • Glacier Point Road is the priority. Three of the best stops sit on it and it's a 45–60 min climb each way — go up once, hit all three, come down.
  • Best light is late afternoon at Glacier Point / Washburn; midday is fine for the valley shots.
  • Fuel up before the hills. No gas in Yosemite Valley — nearest is Crane Flat, El Portal, or back in Mariposa.
  • Cell signal is patchy on Glacier Point Road — screenshot directions and download an offline map before you head up.
  • Bring layers + water. Glacier Point Road tops out around 7,000+ ft and runs cooler than the valley.
  • Restrooms are at the Lodge, Glacier Point, and the Taft Point trailhead — not at the roadside viewpoints.
